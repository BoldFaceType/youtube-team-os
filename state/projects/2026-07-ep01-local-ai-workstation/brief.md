**Selected: Candidate 1.** It carries the build narrative *and* the benchmark payoff in one video, has the strongest thumbnail hook (a price number + a recognizable GPU), and is the most repurposable into Shorts.

## Core Angle
Take a $200 used enterprise workstation, drop in a used RTX 3090, and prove it runs real local LLMs (up to 70B quantized) fast enough to replace paid cloud AI — for under the price of a year of subscriptions.

## Target Viewer
Budget-minded AI tinkerers and self-hosters, 25–45, who have played with ChatGPT/Claude and want to run models locally for privacy, cost, or curiosity. They know what an LLM is and roughly what a GPU does, but they don't know that a used 3090's 24GB of VRAM is the single cheapest ticket into serious local inference. They want a concrete, buyable parts list and honest numbers — not hype.

## Hook
Open on the ugly beige-black corporate tower and the price tag: "This cost me $200 on eBay. By the end of this video it's running a 70-billion-parameter AI model in my basement — and it cost less than one year of a ChatGPT Plus subscription." Then hard-cut to the LM Studio tokens/sec counter flying. The tension: can a machine an office threw away really replace the cloud?

## Key Points (3–5)
1. **Why the P520 is the sleeper base**: workstation PSU + spare power connectors + tool-less bays mean it takes a 3090 with zero adapters — unlike consumer prebuilts. Sourcing tips + what to avoid (the underpowered PSU variants).
2. **The used RTX 3090 value case**: 24GB VRAM is the number that matters for LLMs; how to buy used safely (memory-junction temps, ex-mining cards, fan test).
3. **The build**: cooling headroom, the one PSU cable gotcha, BIOS / Resizable BAR, driver setup — the parts that actually trip people up.
4. **LM Studio benchmarks (the payoff)**: real tokens/sec across model sizes (7B, 13B, 34B, 70B quantized) with GPU-offload settings, plus which quant to pick for 24GB.
5. **The verdict + total cost**: full bill of materials, cost vs. a new build and vs. cloud subscriptions, and who should NOT do this.

## Call to Action
Ask viewers to comment which model they'd run first on a rig like this (drop a specific model name), and link a pinned parts-list doc + the LM Studio config in the description. Subscribe prompt tied to a planned follow-up: fine-tuning on this same machine.
