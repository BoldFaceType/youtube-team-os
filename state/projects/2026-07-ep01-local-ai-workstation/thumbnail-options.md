- Retention-peak frame (from edit notes): the animated tokens/sec chart during the benchmark section

## Concept A — "The Price Tag" (text-forward)
Visual: The P520 tower shot on a clean dark background, three-quarter angle, with a big glowing "$200" price sticker slapped on the front like a garage-sale tag. A red arrow points from the tower to a floating RTX 3090 card. No face.
Text overlay: "$200 AI PC"
Emotion: Curiosity + disbelief (that number can't be right).
Colors: Dark charcoal background + high-vis yellow-green ($ text) with a red arrow.
Why it works: Leads with the single most clickable fact — the price — and the arrow tells the whole build story at a glance.

## Concept B — "The Reveal" (face-forward)
Visual: Creator's face on the left, eyebrows-up "did this actually work?" expression, lit hard. On the right, the tower with the 3090 installed and a phone/monitor showing a chat response mid-generation. A green "70B" badge glows on the GPU.
Text overlay: "RUNS 70B LOCAL"
Emotion: Shock / FOMO (you didn't know used hardware could do this).
Colors: Teal-to-black gradient background + orange face key light for pop; green "70B" badge as the contrast accent.
Why it works: The human expression drives the click; the "70B LOCAL" claim is the specific, unusual payoff most competitors can't show on cheap hardware.

## Concept C — "Cloud vs Basement" (comparison)
Visual: Split thumbnail. Left half: a greyed-out ChatGPT-style cloud logo with a "$$$/yr" strike-through. Right half: the P520 tower glowing with the 3090, a small tokens/sec counter overlaid. A bold white divider line down the middle.
Text overlay: "CLOUD vs $200 PC"
Emotion: Curiosity + desire (I want to stop paying).
Colors: Muted grey (cloud side) vs saturated blue-and-green (PC side) — the contrast itself is the message.
Why it works: Frames the video as a money/independence story, which broadens appeal beyond hardware nerds to anyone tired of subscriptions.

## Recommended
Concept A — "The Price Tag." It front-loads the strongest, most unambiguous hook (the $200 number) that pairs directly with the title's value promise, reads instantly at small size with almost no clutter, and needs no reliance on facial expression to land. Concept B is the strongest A/B test challenger because the face + "70B LOCAL" targets a different curiosity gap (capability rather than price).
